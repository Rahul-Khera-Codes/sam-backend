# Market Agent — Making reports actually relevant to your industry

*Proposal in response to Sam's feedback (Jul 9): "This report needs to be relevant to the business, its own industry and market" + "what is the prompt?"*

## Why the reports feel generic right now

Market Agent is supposed to research trends specific to each business's own industry. But the only "industry" info it has today is a simple dropdown in Business Settings (options like Restaurant, Professional, Landscaping, Other). Many real businesses — including Divinity DJs — don't have a matching option, so they're stuck on "Other." When the system tries to research "trends in the Other industry," that means nothing useful, so the report comes back generic instead of specific to DJs/events.

This isn't the AI ignoring your business — it's that it's never actually been told what industry you're in beyond a vague label.

## What we're proposing

**1. Show what each card is actually researching (answers "what is the prompt?")**
Each of the 7 analyst cards (Trend, Cultural, Market Research, etc.) gets a small (i) info icon. Hovering shows the plain-English version of what that card searched for — including your business's industry, so it's obvious at a glance whether it's using something specific and useful, or a generic placeholder.

**2. Add a real way to describe your industry, and prompt you to fill it in**
Add a free-text field — "Describe your business/industry" — alongside the dropdown, and have Market Agent use that instead. If it's empty or still generic, show a clear on-page notice telling you exactly what to fill in and why ("Add a business description so your reports are tailored to your industry"), instead of silently producing generic content.

**3. Already available today: Custom Analysts**
Separately — you can already add your own "Custom Analyst" on the Market Agent page and write your own research focus in plain English (e.g., "watch for pricing trends among wedding/event DJs in Ontario"). This works right now and doesn't need any of the above — worth using in the meantime for anything the built-in 6 analysts aren't nailing.
